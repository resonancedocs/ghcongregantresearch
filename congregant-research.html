Congregant Research

After speaking with nearly every staff member and completing the communication audit, we conducted research on the congregation to understand Greenhouse from their perspective. We did this in two ways:

1. A survey of the congregation
2. Focus groups where we could directly interact with attendees

The survey generated 278 responses, providing a broad view of how people engage with the church, where they are connecting, and where gaps exist. While respondents tend to be more engaged than the average attendee, the data gives a clear picture of how committed congregants experience Greenhouse — and where the current systems are working or breaking down.

To complement this, we conducted two focus groups of 10 people each. These groups were intentionally balanced between newer attendees (less than three years) and longer-term attendees (more than three years), allowing us to compare early impressions with long-term experience. Seventeen of the 20 participants were women, and the average age was in the late 30s, with representation ranging from early 20s to late 50s. These conversations were conducted in December 2025.

Together, these two inputs provide both scale and depth. The survey identifies patterns across the congregation, while the focus groups reveal the lived experience behind those patterns — what people actually feel, where they struggle, and why they stay.

What emerges from both is consistent: Greenhouse is strong in its spiritual environment, teaching, and mission clarity, but there are clear and repeated gaps in how people form relationships, navigate next steps, and stay connected over time. These are not isolated issues. They are systemic patterns reflected across both datasets.
